About Us

The Cybersecurity Laboratory is a security testing environment for computer programs and devices, using defense tools and ethical hacking. Its objective is to contribute to the development, research and innovation in security technologies and applications. It serves for training and support to graduate students and researchers as a test bed to develop, test and evaluate cybersecurity tools, procedures and controls. Cybersecurity today has become an indispensable area for multiple disciplines. The lines of research that emerge from here are related to multiple areas such as:

  • Cryptography
  • Data mining
  • Protocols
  • Digital Design and Verification, among others

The laboratory has a physical and virtual space to detect, test and exploit vulnerabilities. The physical laboratory is made up of 12 workstations while the virtual laboratory is made up of various virtual machines with the most commonly used operating systems (Windows in various versions, Linux in various distributions and versions, and Android in various versions). vulnerable virtual machines against which tests and attacks can be developed. The workstations (virtual and physical) have specialized software for penetration testing and investigation such as Kali Linux, Parrot OS and Microsoft Windows.

We carry out research on specific topics related to Cybersecurity in which the lines of research developed in the Coordination of Computational Sciences are linked.

Consulting on security, including databases, information management, encryption, authentication and storage among others.
